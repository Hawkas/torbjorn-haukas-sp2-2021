How it works

What are NFTs?

NFTs are non-fungible tokens.

Non-fungible means that it's unique and can't be replaced with something else.

More technically, NFT is a unit of data on a digital ledger called a blockchain, where each NFT represents a unique digital item.

Essentially, NFTs allow people to “own” images, gifs, audio, videos, items in video games, and other forms of creative work. They can really be anything digital, but all of the current excitement is around using the tech to sell digital art.

Examples of NFTs

The first-ever tweet by Jack Dorsey was sold as an NFT for the equivalent of $2.9 million.

The original Nyan Cat was sold for the equivalent of $600,000.

And the single most expensive NFT ever sold — Everydays: The First 5000 Days. A collage of every single artwork made by the digital artist Beeple. It was sold as an NFT for $69.3 million.

Everyone can download these artworks for free, so why?

That's right, you can. But NFTs are designed to give you something that can't be copied: ownership of the work.

To put it in terms of physical art collecting: anyone can buy Nyan Cat. But only one person can own the original.

Owning something exclusive is often a reward in and of itself, and it could even be resold later for an even higher value. This is what makes NFTs so attractive.
